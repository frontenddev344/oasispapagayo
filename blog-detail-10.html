Australia's Coastline: Where to Start and Stop

Australia's vast coastline offers some of the world's most stunning beaches, rugged cliffs, and unique marine life. Here are key spots to include on your journey.

The Great Ocean Road, Victoria

This iconic coastal drive features the famous Twelve Apostles rock formations, rainforests, and seaside towns. It's perfect for scenic stops, hikes, and wildlife spotting.

Byron Bay, New South Wales

Known for its relaxed vibe, Byron Bay offers excellent surfing, beaches, and a lively arts scene. It's a great spot to unwind and connect with local culture.

The Whitsundays, Queensland

A group of 74 islands surrounded by the Great Barrier Reef. Crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches make this area a paradise for snorkelling, sailing, and relaxation.

Margaret River, Western Australia

Famous for its vineyards, caves, and rugged coastline, this region is ideal for food lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Tasmania's East Coast

Wild beaches, national parks, and quiet towns offer a peaceful escape with abundant wildlife and natural beauty.
